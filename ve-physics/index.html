How do they work?

 

TWO CLASSES :

 

1. High-precision engines

    e.g. animated movies

           scientific simulations

2. Real-time engines

    e.g. video games

 

hi-PRECISION

  • Significant amounts of processing power req. to calculate very precise physics.
  • Time taken to calculate the next frame of the simulation can last from: minutes to days.

    

REAL-TIME

  • Speed of processors and game-play over simulation accuracy.
  • Designed to use "approximations" - simplifying calculations & decreasing accuracy to make computations 'on time'.

Rigid-body

  • Most common
  • Less CPU intensive
  • Easily configurable
  • Objects simulated as a whole with different categories of objects

SOFT-BODY

TWO BASE TYPES OF PHYSICS SIMULATIONS

  • Very CPU intensive
  • Easily modified
  • Objects simulated as a sum of their parts, thus 'harder to work with'.
  • Behaviour of objects emerges from the behaviour of it's 'parts'

Difficulties & challenges

1.

Physics simulations are non-trivial

What do you mean non-trivial?

FLUID DYNAMICS

What makes it complex?

  • Water is denser than air. It flows and changes in a way that developers can animate but often not actually model.
  • Things with a greater density are going to sink, while things with lighter density will float.
     
  • Difficult to program: Humans underwater, since the amount of air in the lungs affect buoyancy.

Let's talk waves

What makes it complex?

  • Waves break when the bottom of the wave slows down due to friction on the reef or sand and the top of the wave overtakes it, spilling over the top.
     
  • Problem : this action changes the wave from the simple crest-and-trough formation to a more complex one with different parts moving at different speeds.
     
  • Processing power  required is immense.

RESOURCE AND TIME-BASED CONSTRAINTS

 

Hence: approximations taken instead

OTher precision issues:

Entities may be : 

 

  1. Positioned, scaled and rotated based on numerical values that aren't always integers. Accuracy is lost if rounding occurs mid-calculations.
    Issue : objects scaled inappropriately or placed wrongly, breaking immersion
     
  2. Special variable types might be used e.g. long, double and it may take double the time in length for calculations to be completed on machines that do not support them.
    Issue : increased load on processing units

3.

HARDWARE "Resource" limitations

About calculations...

Well...

  1. Issue : Calculation speed impacting game-play
    Actions that are expected to take up a short amount of time, actually take longer because of latency during calculations.
     
  2. Objects requiring a large amount of calculations would need hardware capable of making such computations on demand, especially in real-time.

    Issue : It's expensive. Leading to trade-offs between performance and costs
